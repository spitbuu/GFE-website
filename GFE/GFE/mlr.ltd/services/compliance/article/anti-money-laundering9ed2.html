Anti-money laundering

Our anti-money laundering (AML) compliance service is specially designed for the banking and financial sector, with a particular focus on AML officers working for Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs). We offer a comprehensive range of solutions to help financial institutions comply with international and local AML regulations, identify and mitigate money laundering risks, and strengthen the security of their financial operations.

Key International Law Texts

  1. EU AML Directives: The European Union’s directives on Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorist Financing (CFT) provide a legal framework for the prevention, detection, and investigation of money laundering activities.
  2. United States Bank Secrecy Act (BSA): This act imposes obligations on financial institutions to report suspicious transactions, maintain records, and identify clients.
  3. FATF Recommendations: The Financial Action Task Force (FATF) proposes international standards and recommendations to combat money laundering and terrorist financing.

Our Anti-Money Laundering Compliance Services Include

  • Risk Assessment: We conduct a comprehensive assessment of money laundering risks specific to VASPs, including cryptocurrency and other virtual asset transactions.
  • Policies and Procedures: We help implement AML policies that comply with international standards, including Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures, transaction monitoring, and reporting of suspicious activities.
  • Technology and Monitoring: We implement advanced technological solutions for detecting suspicious transactions and real-time data analysis.
  • Training Programs: We offer regular and in-depth training sessions for AML officers, covering the latest techniques and regulatory requirements.
  • Specialized Workshops: We organize intensive workshops on fraud detection for virtual assets, identifying suspicious transactions, and analyzing money laundering patterns.
  • Professional Certifications: We prepare for internationally recognized professional certifications in AML compliance.

Why Is It Important?

  • Regulatory Compliance: Helps VASPs comply with AML/CFT requirements, reducing the risk of regulatory and legal sanctions.
  • Risk Reduction: Proactively identifies and mitigates money laundering risks associated with virtual asset transactions.
  • Reputation and Trust: Enhances reputation and trust among clients, partners, and regulators through robust compliance practices.
  • Market Access: AML compliance is often a prerequisite to operate in certain markets and establish financial partnerships.
